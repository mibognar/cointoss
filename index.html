Instructions

In this experiment you will have to play the cointoss game 600 times with a 1EUR coin that has its heads marked with an 'X' and tails marked with an 'O'. During the experiment you will see a screen that will count your tosses and record your results. You also need to record your tosses with the camera of your computer. During this experiment you shall not refresh your browser window, but you can minimize it while you setup or upload your camera recordings. You will need to use a 1EUR coin during this experiment. First, you need to mark your 1EUR coin with a visible marker as seen below:

Mark the heads (there is a country specific drawing on this side) of your coin with an X
Mark the tails ('1 EURO' is written on this side) of your coin with an O
During the experiment you will have to toss the coin and catch it with one hand with an open palm. The experiment page will ask you to report the toss result with a keypress ('X' if heads or 'O' if tails), then start a new toss with the same side up as your last toss' result. See the images below:
Starting position TAILS
Toss resulted in HEADS
New starting position HEADS


If the toss fell on the ground, or it flipped after the landing, mark the toss as a 'toss error' by pressing 'SPACE'.

PRESS 'NEXT' if you have understood the above instructions.
During the coin toss sequences you need to record with your computer's camera. The camera has to be set up in a way that the coin starting position (on your thumb) and the landing position (in your palm) can be seen on the screen.

The test will be separated into 6 sequences with 100 tosses in each. You will need to start the camera recording at the start of the sequences, and stop it at the end. At the end of every sequences you will see a link where you can upload your video. Depending on your speed one sequence (100 tosses) will take approximately 10 minutes. Please be aware that the filesize of videos must be under 1GB and the file format must be '.mp4' or '.mov'.

Before starting the experiment make sure you have an application on your computer that can record .mp4 or .mov videos (for example the 'Camera' application on Windows 10 or MacOS). If you suspect that your videos might be too big, try decreasing the default resolution of the recording.

You should not show your face on camera and you do not need to narrate your tosses. You only need to make sure that all toss starting positions and landing positions are visible on camera. PRESS 'NEXT' if you have understood the above instructions.
